Hello World!

Our world, planet Earth, has been translating around the Sun for over 4.5 billion years ago. The surface of this blue planet is about 3/4 covered with water and the rest is land in larger or smaller extensions such as continents or little islands.

Hello Venus!

Venus is the closest planet to the Earth and that's why often it can be seen by the human naked eye.

Hello Mars!

This planet is the favorite of sci-fi writers as we can see martians in many fiction movies and books. It has a particular red color and it is the actual focus of a potential planet migration in the future.

Hello Mercury!

The smallest planet of the Solar System. Also it is the closest planet to the Sun and, therefore, the hottest.

Hello Moon!

It's the only Earth's natural satellite. It takes 28 days to orbit the planet and it can be seen generally at night

Hello Pluto!

Pluto was considered the ninth planet of the Solar System until the formal definition of "planet" in 2006. Now it is considered a dwarf planet

Fun fact: the planets of the Solar System were named like roman gods. Here is again the list with the corresponding god of each planet:

  • Mercury: God of messengers, travel, and/or commerce.
  • Venus: Goddess of romance and lust.
  • Mars: God of War.
  • Earth (Terra): Godess of the earth
  • Jupiter: God of the sky and lightning.
  • Saturn: God of Agriculture.
  • Uranus (Caelus): Primordial god of the Sky.
  • Neptune: God of the Sea.
